Whole Grain Pasta & Tomato Sauce

Pasta and red sauce - is there a simpler staple recipe than that? Often times I'll find myself absolutely craving this dish but wishing that it was more elevated. That being said, making a delicious sauce from scratch can be time consuming. That is, until I found this recipe.

While the whole grain pasta keeps the dish health conscious, the star of the show here is the simple yet decadent pasta sauce. Taking a few staple ingredients for any good pasta sauce, this recipe uses capers and artichokes to really take the flavors to the next level. Rest assured, the artichokes are cheap and a little goes a long way with the capers. I hope you enjoy as much as I do!

Ingredients

  • 6 oz Whole Grain Radiator Pasta
  • 2 cloves Garlic
  • 1 Red Onion
  • 1 Tbsp Capers
  • ¼ cup Marinated Artichoke Hearts
  • 4 oz Grape Tomatoes
  • 2 Tbsps Crème Fraîche (or 1½ Tbsps Sour Cream)
  • ¼ cup Grated Parmesan Cheese
  • 1 8-oz can Tomato Sauce
  • ¼ tsp Crushed Red Pepper Flakes

Instructions

  1. Prepare the ingredients
    • Fill a medium pot 3/4 of the way up with salted water; cover and heat to boiling on high.
    • Wash and dry the fresh produce.
    • Halve, peel, and thinly slice the onion.
    • Halve the tomatoes.
    • Peel and roughly cop 2 cloves of garlic.
    • Halve the artichokes lengthwise.
  2. Cook the pasta
    • Add the pasta to the pot of boiling water. Cook, stirring occasionally, 8 to 10 minutes, or until al dente. Turn off the heat.
    • Reserving ½ cup of the pasta cooking water, drain thoroughly and return to the pot
  3. Make the sauce
    • In a medium pan (nonstick, if you have one), heat a drizzle of olive oil on medium-high until hot.
    • Add the sliced onion in an even layer. Cook, without stirring, 4 to 5 minutes, or until lightly browned.
    • Add the halved tomatoes, chopped garlic and as much of the red pepper flakes as you'd like, depending on how spicy you'd like the dish to be. Cook, stirring frequently, 1 to 2 minutes, or until softened.
    • Add the tomato sauce (carefully, as the liquid may splatter); season with salt and pepper. Reduce the heat to low and cook, stirring frequently, 1 to 2 minutes, or until combined.
    • Turn off the heat.
  4. Finish the pasta
    • To the pot of cooked pasta, add the sauce, capers, halved artichokes, and half the reserved pasta cooking water. Cook on medium-high, stirring constantly, 1 to 2 minutes, or until the pasta is coated (if necessary, gradually add the remaining cooking water to ensure the pasta is thoroughly coated).
    • Turn off the heat; stir in the crème fraîche until combined
    • Garnish with parmesan; enjoy!
